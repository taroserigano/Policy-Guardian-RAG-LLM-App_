EMPLOYEE LEAVE POLICY
Effective Date: January 1, 2026
Last Updated: January 1, 2026

1. ANNUAL LEAVE

1.1 Entitlement
All full-time employees are entitled to 20 days of paid annual leave per calendar year. Part-time employees receive pro-rated leave based on their contracted hours.

1.2 Accrual
Annual leave accrues at a rate of 1.67 days per month of continuous service. Leave can be taken once it has accrued.

1.3 Request Process
Employees must submit leave requests through the HR portal at least 2 weeks in advance for planned absences. Manager approval is required before leave can be confirmed.

1.4 Carryover
Unused annual leave may be carried over to the next calendar year, up to a maximum of 5 days. Any leave exceeding this limit will be forfeited unless approved by senior management.

1.5 Payment on Termination
Upon termination of employment, employees will be paid for any accrued but unused annual leave up to the date of termination.

2. SICK LEAVE

2.1 Entitlement
Employees are entitled to 10 days of paid sick leave per calendar year. This leave is intended for personal illness, injury, or medical appointments.

2.2 Medical Certificates
A medical certificate is required for sick leave absences exceeding 3 consecutive working days. Employees must provide the certificate within 5 working days of returning to work.

2.3 Notification
Employees must notify their direct supervisor as soon as reasonably possible on the first day of illness, preferably before their scheduled start time.

2.4 Non-Accumulation
Unused sick leave does not accumulate from year to year and cannot be paid out upon termination.

3. PARENTAL LEAVE

3.1 Maternity Leave
Female employees are entitled to 16 weeks of maternity leave. The first 8 weeks are paid at 100% of base salary. The remaining 8 weeks are unpaid unless statutory benefits apply.

3.2 Paternity Leave
Male employees are entitled to 2 weeks of paid paternity leave to be taken within 8 weeks of the birth or adoption.

3.3 Notice Requirements
Employees must provide at least 8 weeks' notice before the intended start date of parental leave, except in cases of premature birth or adoption.

4. COMPASSIONATE LEAVE

4.1 Immediate Family
Employees are entitled to up to 5 days of paid compassionate leave in the event of the death of an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent, sibling).

4.2 Extended Family
Up to 2 days of paid leave is available for the death of extended family members (grandparents, in-laws, aunts, uncles).

4.3 Additional Unpaid Leave
Additional unpaid compassionate leave may be granted at management discretion for exceptional circumstances.

5. STUDY LEAVE

5.1 Eligibility
Employees who have completed at least 2 years of continuous service may apply for study leave for work-related courses or qualifications.

5.2 Approval Process
Applications must be submitted to HR at least 3 months in advance, including course details, costs, and relevance to current role.

5.3 Leave Allocation
Up to 5 days of paid study leave may be granted per year for approved courses. Additional unpaid leave may be considered.

5.4 Financial Assistance
The company may reimburse up to 75% of course fees upon successful completion, subject to budget availability and continuing employment for at least 12 months after completion.

6. PUBLIC HOLIDAYS

6.1 Recognized Holidays
The company observes all federal and state public holidays. A list of recognized holidays is published annually.

6.2 Work on Public Holidays
Employees required to work on public holidays will receive time-and-a-half pay or an alternative day off, at the employee's choice.

7. UNPAID LEAVE

7.1 Circumstances
Unpaid leave may be granted for personal reasons, extended travel, or other circumstances not covered by other leave provisions.

7.2 Application
Requests must be submitted at least 4 weeks in advance and are subject to operational requirements and manager approval.

7.3 Impact on Benefits
Extended unpaid leave (exceeding 4 weeks) may affect accrual of annual leave and other employment benefits.

8. LEAVE DURING NOTICE PERIOD

8.1 Annual Leave
Employees are encouraged to take accrued annual leave during their notice period. The company reserves the right to direct employees to take leave during notice.

8.2 Other Leave Types
Sick leave and other special leave provisions continue to apply during the notice period.

9. ABUSE OF LEAVE

9.1 False Claims
Making false claims for sick leave or other leave entitlements is considered serious misconduct and may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

9.2 Excessive Absences
Patterns of excessive or suspicious absences may trigger a review and potential disciplinary action.

10. POLICY REVIEW

This policy is reviewed annually and may be updated to reflect changes in legislation, business needs, or best practices. Employees will be notified of any significant changes.

For questions or clarifications regarding this policy, please contact the Human Resources Department.
